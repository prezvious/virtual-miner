## Event System Concept

The **EVENT SYSTEM** for Virtual Miner should not feel like a slot machine popup that only says `+200% ore for 20s`.
It should feel like **the mine itself is alive**: sometimes it helps, sometimes it bargains, sometimes it rewrites the rules of geology, commerce, time, or gravity for a short moment.

### Core Direction
- Events should be **rule-benders**, not just stat buffs.
- Each event should create a **story headline** the player remembers and sees again in the offline report.
- Events should interact with **depth, rarity, storage, contracts, selling, biomes, and player behavior**.
- The best events should make the player think: `Do I click harder, hold my ore, sell now, go deeper, or take the risk?`
- Keep the classic events like `Rich Vein`, `Gold Rush`, `Earthquake`, and `Meteor Strike` as the baseline pool.
- Add the following as the **signature event pool** that gives Virtual Miner its own identity.

## Signature Mine Events (Up to 20)

**1. Surveyor's Ghostline (Uncommon, 25s)** — A phantom expedition route appears across the rock face. Ores mined exactly along the glowing line gain stacking rarity pressure and depth bonus.  
**Main concept:** A temporary precision mini-game inside an idle miner. The player is rewarded for discipline, not just speed.

**2. Reverse Cave-In (Common, instant)** — Instead of burying the player, a collapse violently spits hidden rubble upward from deeper layers. Gain a burst of ore from one biome deeper than your current zone.  
**Main concept:** Turn disaster into treasure delivery. A cave-in becomes an elevator from the underworld.

**3. Market Eclipse (Uncommon, 30s)** — The market suddenly values humble material over luxury ore. Common to Rare ores sell for absurd prices, while high-rarity ore gives normal or reduced value.  
**Main concept:** Flip the economy upside down so “trash loot” becomes the jackpot for a moment.

**4. Fossil Verdict (Uncommon, instant choice)** — An ancient buried tribunal judges your recent behavior: active clicking, hoarding, auto-selling, or deep pushing. It grants a boon based on your playstyle.  
**Main concept:** A reactive event that mirrors the player’s habits back at them.

**5. Moonwell Breach (Rare, 20s)** — A circular tear opens in the shaft and leaks ore from the next biome band into the current mine. Future loot appears early, but only for a short window.  
**Main concept:** A teaser event that lets players “taste the future” before fully reaching it.

**6. Black Lantern Parade (Rare, 25s)** — Spectral miners march through the tunnels carrying ore carts. If the player keeps mining rhythm steady, the parade drops extra cargo with every successful cadence streak.  
**Main concept:** A ghost convoy event built around tempo and spectacle.

**7. Cathedral Resonance (Rare, 20s)** — Every click produces a note. A steady rhythm awakens the cave into a singing chamber, multiplying ore output and rare-drop pressure.  
**Main concept:** The mine becomes a musical instrument; rhythm becomes profit.

**8. Time-Lag Pocket (Rare, 35s)** — All ore mined during the event is suspended in a temporal bubble and delivered at the end, multiplied by the amount of depth gained while the bubble was active.  
**Main concept:** Delayed gratification. The player builds suspense, then gets a dramatic payout.

**9. Vein Lottery Office (Rare, instant choice)** — Three sealed shaft permits appear. One is a safe reward, one is a cursed-but-rich gamble, and one is pure chaos. The player must choose immediately.  
**Main concept:** Controlled gambling with personality, not generic RNG.

**10. Gravity Lease (Epic, 15s)** — The mine offers a strange bargain: convert current depth into rarity boost, or sacrifice rarity boost for a giant depth lunge.  
**Main concept:** Let the player trade one progression axis for another in real time.

**11. Glitch Fault (Epic, 20s)** — One ore type begins duplicating itself on every drop, but all duplicated ore decays if it is not sold before the event ends.  
**Main concept:** A panic-profit event that creates urgency and storage pressure.

**12. Crownroot Bloom (Epic, 30s)** — Living mineral roots invade the cargo hold. Empty storage slots begin to grow crystal fruit that can be harvested for premium value, but overfilling kills the bloom.  
**Main concept:** Reward restraint and storage management instead of endless hoarding.

**13. Furnace Marriage (Epic, 25s)** — The mine’s heat aligns perfectly and pairs of low-tier ores fuse into alloy forms one or two rarity tiers higher.  
**Main concept:** Temporary ore alchemy that makes even basic drops exciting.

**14. Silence Protocol (Epic, 20s)** — All manual mining is disabled, but workers, drills, bots, and conveyor systems operate at extreme efficiency.  
**Main concept:** An anti-click event that still feels amazing, especially for automation-heavy players.

**15. Debt Collector from Below (Epic, 2-minute contract)** — A subterranean banker offers instant gold right now, but demands repayment in specific ores before the timer ends. Success grants a massive bonus; failure applies interest.  
**Main concept:** High-pressure economic pact instead of a passive buff.

**16. Meteor Script (Legendary, 12s)** — Burning star-runes carve symbols into the cavern walls. Clicking the correct sequence awards Celestial fragments, Arcane ore, or a Mythic-tier pulse.  
**Main concept:** A jackpot event that behaves like a short puzzle, not just free loot.

**17. Hollow Sun Bore (Legendary, 18s)** — A radiant shaft opens to a molten daylight chamber. Heat converts low-tier ore into premium ore, but overheating burns the value away.  
**Main concept:** Risk-managed thermal jackpot with a visible danger meter.

**18. Echo Quarry (Legendary, 20s)** — Ghost miners replay your last 10 seconds of actions at 150% efficiency. Fast, smart play before the event becomes even more valuable during it.  
**Main concept:** Time replay that weaponizes the player’s own skill.

**19. Burial Auction (Legendary, 30s)** — A wandering relic broker appears and auctions one impossible artifact. Bidding is done with ore combinations, not gold, forcing players to choose what to sacrifice.  
**Main concept:** A prestige-feeling market event with meaningful resource decisions.

**20. Singularity Rehearsal (Mythic / Jackpot, 10s)** — The mine folds inward. UI distorts, gravity bends, drop tables compress upward, and one impossible tier can appear outside the normal pity rhythm.  
**Main concept:** A page-level reality-break event. This should feel like “the whole game just held its breath.”

## Strong Event Families

- **Route Events**: events that reward movement discipline or mining pattern.
- **Economy Events**: events that invert sell value, storage logic, or ore relevance.
- **Pact Events**: events that ask the player to choose, wager, or accept risk.
- **Reality Break Events**: events that temporarily rewrite mine rules, physics, or time.
- **Jackpot Spectacle Events**: ultra-rare events that should feel like a once-in-a-session headline.

## Best Design Rule

The best Virtual Miner events should make players say:

`"Wait, the mine is doing WHAT right now?"`

That is the emotional target.
